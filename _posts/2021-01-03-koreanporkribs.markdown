Korean pork ribs are usually marinaded in a spicy sauce. We tried a non-spicy version
and our toddler loved it! We were actually very surprised at how much he enjoyed
chewing the meat off the ribs. This recipe takes a couple hours. Baking the
ribs for a long time at a low heat makes the meat much more tender. The meat
just falls off the bone! We use less sugar and soy sauce the the original recipe
but the ribs were still very delicious!

{% highlight ruby %}
Ingredients:
  ~ 1 rack of baby back pork ribs

  Marinade:
    1 sweet onion
    2 1 Korean pear or Fuji apple
    5 cloves garlic, peeled
    1 inch piece ginger
    1/2 cup firmly packed dark brown sugar
    1/2 cup soy sauce
    1/2 cup water
    2 tablespoons dark sesame oil

{% endhighlight %}

{% highlight ruby %}
Directions:
  1. Mince onion, pear, garlic, ginger and add in the sugar
     soy sauce and water
  2. Place the meat and marinade in the fridge for 8-24 hours
  3. Bake for 2 hours at 300 degrees fahrenheit
  4. Coat the ribs with the left over marinade and broil for 10 minutes

{% endhighlight %}
